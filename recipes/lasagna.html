Lasagna recipe

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made from layers of pasta sheets, alternating with fillings such as ragù (meat sauce), cheese, and béchamel sauce. It's baked until bubbly and golden, offering a harmonious blend of flavors and textures. Lasagna can vary in its ingredients and can also be made vegetarian or with various other fillings.

ingredients:

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef or pork (or a mix of both)
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • Garlic cloves, minced
  • Canned crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Grated mozzarella cheese
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Béchamel sauce (made from butter, flour, milk, and a pinch of nutmeg)
  • Olive oil
  • Red wine (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Dried Italian herbs or oregano (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions and minced garlic, sautéing until translucent.
  3. Add the ground beef or pork to the skillet and cook until browned. Drain excess fat if necessary.
  4. Pour in the crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce, and if using, the red wine. Season with salt, pepper, and dried Italian herbs or oregano. Let the sauce simmer for about 20-30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix together the ricotta cheese with a handful of grated Parmesan and some chopped basil. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  6. Prepare the béchamel sauce in another pot by melting butter, adding flour to make a roux, and gradually whisking in the milk until smooth. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg. Cook until thickened.
  7. Boil the lasagna noodles according to package instructions until al dente, then drain and set aside.
  8. Begin layering the lasagna in a baking dish. Start with a layer of meat sauce at the bottom, followed by a layer of lasagna noodles. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture, then some béchamel sauce, and sprinkle a layer of mozzarella cheese.
  9. Repeat the layering process until all ingredients are used up, finishing with a layer of meat sauce, béchamel, and a generous sprinkle of both mozzarella and Parmesan cheese on top.
  10. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 30-40 minutes or until the lasagna is bubbly and the top is golden brown.
  11. Once done, remove from the oven and let it rest for 10 minutes to set.
  12. Garnish with fresh parsley and additional grated Parmesan if desired.
  13. Serve warm and enjoy!
